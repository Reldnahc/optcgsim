### 02-engine-mechanics.s010 (Turn structure)
Turn player may repeatedly:

- Play a Character, Stage, or `[Main]` Event from hand.
- Activate `[Activate: Main]` effects.
- Give active DON!! to Leader or Characters.
- Declare an attack, if legal.
- End the phase.

Neither player can attack on their first turn.

### 02-engine-mechanics.s037 (First-turn restrictions)
| Player / turn | Draw Phase | DON!! Phase | Attack |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Player going first, first turn | No draw | Place only 1 DON!! | Cannot attack |
| Player going second, first turn | Draw normally | Place 2 DON!! if available | Cannot attack |

## Scope
- Implement `Action.declareAttack` legality for active-player attackers in Main / battle entry, respecting first-turn restrictions and summoning sickness.
- Support vanilla attack targets: opponent Leader and rested opposing Characters where legal.
- Implement vanilla damage resolution: leader damage moves top life to hand; empty life causes loss at rule processing.
- Implement vanilla character K.O. from legal attacks and move K.O.'d characters to trash with required attached DON return behavior.
- Complete milestone terminal paths needed for vanilla matches: damage-based loss and deck-out loss, alongside existing concession handling.

## Out of Scope
- Counter step cards, blockers, and battle power modification windows.
- Full triggered-effect runtime during battle or damage processing.
- Card-specific combat exceptions beyond vanilla legality rules.

## Constraints
- Combat legality must be derived from authoritative state, not client hints.
- Do not invent blocker/counter timing or card-specific battle exceptions inside this vanilla slice.

## Required Tests
- Integration: legal leader attack moves top Life card to hand and updates life count/order.
- Integration: attacking a rested Character K.O.s it and returns attached DON correctly.
- Integration: attack legality is absent on illegal turns/attackers and present for legal vanilla attackers.
- Integration: drawing from empty deck or losing final Life reaches terminal game-over state deterministically.
